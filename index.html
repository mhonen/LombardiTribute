Vince Thomas Lombardi

American Football Coaching Legend.

Early Life

Born in July 11th, 1913 in SheepShead Bay of Brooklyn to Enrico "Harry" Lombardi and Matilda "Mattie" Izzo For highschool was enrolled in Cathederal Preparatory Seminar which was a division of Cathederal College of Immaculate Conception in Brooklyn studying to be a Catholic priest. It was at this time that Lombardi found his love for American football and would play off campus against school policy. Later, wneh he decided he didn;t want to be a priest, he enrolled at St. Francis Preparatory highschool in fall of 1932 where he played for the school football team: The Terriers


Early Career

In 1933, Lombardi recieved a football scholarship with Fordham University in the Bronx. He eventually graduated on June 16th, 1937 but due to the "Great Depression," he could not find any work. He would eventutally play semi professional football with the Washington Clippers and later worked as a debt collector. However, he was later convinced by his father to study Law at Fordham. Despite nearly completing the degree, he dropped out of school due to the desire to find work and start a family.


Coaching Career (Highschool and College)

In 1942, Lombardi became the head coach of St. Cecilia Highschool in Englewood, New Jersey. He would lead the highschool to the top rank in the nation. In 1948, he became the offensive coordinator for the football team a West Point Academy. It is beleived that during this time, he would learn the value of disipline in running a football team which would shape his coaching demeanor in later years.

Professional Coaching Career

In 1954 and the age of 41, Lombardi started is NFL career as an offensive coordinator of the New York Giants Within in three years, he helped the team win the league title against the Chicago Bears. In 1958, Lobardi becmae the head coach and general manager of the struggling Green Bay Packers. After his takeover of the team the Packers record dratically imporoved to 7-5 and Lombardi received Coach "Rookie of the Year" for that year.


Between 1960-66, dispite loosing the 1960 league championship, Lombardi would win three consecutive league championships in 1965, 1966, and 1967. He would also win the first ever two "Superbowls" against the Kansas City Cheifs and the Oakland Raiders.
